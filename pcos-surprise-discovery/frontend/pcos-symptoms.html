What is PCOS?

Polycystic Ovary Syndrome (PCOS) is a hormonal disorder affecting approximately 10% of women of reproductive age. It is characterized by a combination of reproductive and metabolic symptoms.

Diagnostic Criteria

Rotterdam Criteria (2003) ‚Äî Most Widely Used

Diagnosis requires 2 of the following 3 criteria:

  1. Oligo-ovulation or anovulation (irregular or absent ovulation)
  2. Clinical or biochemical hyperandrogenism (elevated male hormones)
  3. Polycystic ovaries on ultrasound

NIH Criteria (1990)

Diagnosis requires both:

  1. Hyperandrogenism (clinical or biochemical)
  2. Chronic anovulation
